What is the Harmony Collective

The Harmony Collective is a Canada-based initiative dedicated to bringing a personalized music experience to rural and inner-city communities, focusing on populations with typically less access to the performing arts -- care homes, veterans’ homes, inner-city households and schools, and homes for those with disabilities.

Our Current Project for COVID-Relief: Musical Pen Pals

Due to the suspension of in-person efforts for community service, Musical Pen Pals (MPP) is our first all-digital initiative for students of all ages.

Musicians will record and upload themselves playing/singing, fill out a reflective questionnaire about their selection, and we will compile submissions monthly as an “audio letter” for underserved and isolated populations. Those who receive a video will fill out reflection questions on their listening experience and their responses will be emailed back to the student musicians, facilitating a digital “pen-pal” experience.

Origins of the Pen-Pal Idea

Having a pen-pal comes with many benefits: it encourages both participants to practice mutual empathy and concern, and helps foster a sense of connection—all good things, especially while COVID-19 has prevented social gatherings from happening.

Why music?

Research has consistently shown positive correlations between music and wellness benefits such as preserving neurological function, improving mood, relieving stress, and forming a sense of community between the parties involved in music-making and recieving.

Why Music?

The team at The Harmony Collective was originally planning a tour across the rural areas of Canada to bring live classical music to communities with limited opportunities to attend concerts. This will be back up for discussion again, post COVID-19!
